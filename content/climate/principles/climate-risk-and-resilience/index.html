Overview

Risk is created when hazards meet exposed and vulnerable systems

Climate risk is not only the presence of a hazard. Risk emerges when people, infrastructure, ecosystems and livelihoods are exposed and vulnerable to climate shocks.

01HazardHeat, drought, flood, fire, storm, landslide or water stress.
02ExposurePeople, assets, forests, settlements and services located in risk zones.
03VulnerabilityWeak preparedness, poverty, degraded ecosystems and poor infrastructure.
04ResilienceThe ability to prepare, absorb, recover and adapt.

For FutureWorld Intelligence, climate resilience means reducing vulnerability through knowledge, local planning, ecosystem restoration, early warning and community-centered action.

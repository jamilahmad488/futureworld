The Global Energy Transition

Balancing energy security, sustainability and economic growth in the twenty-first century.

Executive Summary

Modern societies face a dual challenge: meeting rising energy demand while reducing environmental impacts and strengthening long-term energy security.

Key Drivers

Renewable Technologies

Solar, wind and other renewable technologies are changing the structure of energy systems.

Electrification

Transport, buildings and industry are increasingly linked to electricity-system planning.

Storage and Digital Systems

Storage, smart grids and digital controls are becoming central to reliable transition pathways.

Strategic Challenges

Countries must balance affordability, reliability, industrial competitiveness and environmental sustainability during the transition process.

FutureWorld Assessment

The energy transition is not only an environmental issue. It is also a strategic, economic and geopolitical transformation shaping the future of development.

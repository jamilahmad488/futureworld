## 1. Research and Evidence Review

- [ ] Report has a clear research question or policy question.
- [ ] Core thesis is clearly stated.
- [ ] Major claims are supported by credible sources.
- [ ] Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources are prioritized.
- [ ] Statistics are checked against original sources.
- [ ] Dates and figures are current enough for publication.
- [ ] Evidence, analysis and projection are clearly distinguished.

## 2. Citation Review

- [ ] All major factual claims have nearby citations.
- [ ] All citations are real and accessible.
- [ ] Sources directly support the claims.
- [ ] Full reference list is complete.
- [ ] No AI-generated citation remains unverified.
- [ ] No source is misrepresented or overstated.

## 3. Originality Review

- [ ] Similarity check completed.
- [ ] High-similarity paragraphs rewritten.
- [ ] Direct quotes are short, quoted and cited.
- [ ] Report does not copy source structure too closely.
- [ ] Report does not repeat previous FWI content unnecessarily.

## 4. Policy Neutrality and Language Review

- [ ] Tone is objective, constructive and non-sensational.
- [ ] Negative developments are presented responsibly and proportionately.
- [ ] No country, people, religion, civilization or group is treated as inherently superior or inferior.
- [ ] Analysis avoids propaganda, hate, manipulation and unsupported blame.
- [ ] Uncertainty is acknowledged where required.

## 5. AI Governance Review

- [ ] AI was used only as a support tool.
- [ ] AI-generated claims were verified.
- [ ] AI-generated visuals are not presented as documentary evidence unless based on verified data.
- [ ] Human editor accepts final responsibility.

## 6. Visual and Data Integrity

- [ ] Figures, maps and charts are accurate and labelled.
- [ ] AI-generated visuals are explanatory, not misleading.
- [ ] Data visualizations match source data.
- [ ] Captions do not overstate what the image or data proves.
